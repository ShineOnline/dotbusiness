General questions about AI marketing

Traditional digital marketing relies on humans creating content, managing accounts, responding to reviews, and running campaigns manually — which is time-consuming, inconsistent, and expensive at scale.

AI marketing uses intelligent software agents to do those same tasks automatically, continuously, and across multiple platforms at once. At Dot Business, our AI agents monitor your online presence, generate review requests after every completed job, respond to reviews, publish social content, keep your business listings accurate, and ensure you appear in AI-powered search results — all without requiring your time or attention.

The result is a marketing operation that never sleeps, never forgets, and scales with your business.

AI answer engines are platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Claude that give direct answers to questions rather than showing a list of links. When someone asks "who are the best fire alarm maintenance companies in the Midlands?" — these platforms name specific businesses. They don't show ten blue links. They make a recommendation.

Over 50% of consumers now use AI-powered search to make buying decisions, according to McKinsey & Company. That percentage is growing rapidly. If your business isn't optimised to appear in those answers, you're invisible to a significant and growing portion of your potential customers — regardless of how good your website is.

For field service engineering businesses

This is the most common thing we hear from field service MDs — and it's completely understandable. Referrals have worked for years. The business is running well. Why change?

Here's what's changing whether you engage with it or not. The people making procurement decisions in 2025 are younger, they verify online before they trust, and they use AI platforms to research contractors. Even warm referrals get Googled — and increasingly Gemini'd and ChatGPT'd — before the call is confirmed. A new facilities manager inheriting your contract doesn't have the relationship history your existing contact had. They do their own research.

We've seen excellent field service businesses lose contracts not because their service slipped, but because a competitor looked more credible online. The referral opened the door — the digital presence either confirmed or killed the deal.

Referrals remain your most valuable lead source. AI marketing is what converts them reliably.

Multi-county coverage is actually where AI visibility optimisation delivers the most value — and where most field service businesses have the biggest gaps.

AI platforms treat each county as a separate search market. A business based in Coventry serving Warwickshire, Leicestershire, and Northamptonshire needs to be optimised for all three counties separately. A search for "best HVAC contractors in Leicestershire" and "best HVAC contractors in Northamptonshire" are two completely different queries with potentially different results.

Most field service businesses only have visibility in their home county — invisible in the others even if they physically service them every day.

We create county-specific content, optimise your Google Business Profile service areas correctly, and build location signals across all major directories for every county you serve. For a business operating across three or more counties, this typically represents the single biggest untapped visibility opportunity.

For local businesses

Yes — and in some ways more immediately than for larger businesses.

Local businesses live or die by their local visibility. When someone in your town searches for what you offer — whether on Google, ChatGPT, or by asking a Facebook group — appearing prominently is the difference between a new customer and that customer going to your competitor.

AI marketing for local businesses focuses on exactly this: making sure you appear when and where local customers are searching, building a consistent flow of Google reviews, keeping your information accurate across every platform, and maintaining a social media presence that makes your business look active and trustworthy.

You don't need a large budget or a marketing team. You need the right systems running in the background. That's what we build.

Traditional Google SEO is about ranking in the list of blue links that appears when someone searches. You optimise your website to appear high in that list.

AI search visibility is different. When someone asks ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity a question, they don't get a list of links — they get a direct answer that names specific businesses. The signals that determine who gets named are different to traditional SEO: business listing consistency, review volume and recency, structured data on your website, how often your business is mentioned across the web, and how clearly AI platforms can understand what you do and where you do it.

Most local businesses have invested in Google SEO to some degree. Almost none have optimised for AI search. This represents a significant opportunity for the businesses that move first.

There's no magic number, but research consistently shows that businesses with more than 40 recent reviews significantly outperform those with fewer than 15 — in both search ranking and customer trust.

More important than the total number is recency. Google weights recent reviews heavily. A business with 20 reviews in the last three months will often outrank a business with 100 reviews where the most recent is from 18 months ago.

We automate review generation after every customer interaction — appointment, visit, completed job — so your review count grows consistently month by month without you having to ask anyone manually.

Handling negative reviews well is one of the most underrated reputation management opportunities available to a small business.

Research shows that potential customers read negative reviews almost as carefully as positive ones — and they pay close attention to how the business responds. A thoughtful, professional response to a negative review can actually build trust with prospective customers who see that you take feedback seriously and resolve issues.

We monitor all your reviews across Google, Facebook, and other platforms. When a negative review comes in, we draft a response that acknowledges the concern, addresses it professionally, and invites the customer to resolve it directly. You review and approve before anything goes live.

What we don't do is ignore negative reviews or leave them unanswered — which is unfortunately what most businesses do, and which does real damage to perception.

Our Social Media AI service is designed specifically for business owners who don't have time for social media — which is most of them.

We create a monthly content plan for your business based on your services, your tone, and what performs best in your industry. Our AI agents draft the posts. Our account managers review and refine them. They're scheduled to go out at peak engagement times. You don't write a word or think about it.

The result is a social media presence that looks active, professional, and on-brand — posting consistently three times per week across LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram.

For local businesses, consistent posting matters more than follower count. When a potential customer checks your page before deciding whether to book, recent posts signal that the business is active, credible, and worth contacting. A page with nothing posted in six months sends the opposite message — even if the business is excellent.
